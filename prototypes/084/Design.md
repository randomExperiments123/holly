# Design Document: 084 — Asymmetrical Grid

## Concept
The grid is a starting point, not a cage. Every element is placed with intentional imbalance — creating visual tension that draws the eye across the page in unexpected paths.

## Color Palette
- Background: #f0ede8 (warm paper)
- Text: #1a1a1a (almost black)
- Accent 1: #d62828 (strong red)
- Accent 2: #003049 (deep navy)
- Accent 3: #fcbf49 (amber)

## Typography
- Headings: "Arial", "Helvetica Neue", sans-serif, condensed
- Body: system sans-serif
- Sizes: irregular hierarchy — h1 may be smaller than h2 intentionally

## Interactions
- Elements shift position slightly on hover
- Cards tilt on hover (rotate 2-3deg)
- Overlapping areas have shadow depth
- Staggered entry on scroll

## Layout
- No two sections share the same grid
- Hero: title top-right, subtitle bottom-left
- Projects: masonry-like but deliberately uneven
- About: split vertically at a non-50% ratio (e.g., 35/65)
- Contact: extreme off-center (90% to one side)

## Key Design Decisions
- Warm paper background to contrast with "broken rules" approach
- Red as accent for tension
- Shadows to create depth between overlapping elements
- Every layout choice can be justified but looks "wrong" initially
